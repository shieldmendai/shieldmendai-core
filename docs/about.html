About ShieldMendAi

Infrastructure should fail loudly, safely, and recoverably.

ShieldMendAi began with a practical problem: independent builders and small teams lose time diagnosing silent failures, restarting services, and reconstructing incidents after the fact. The project is building the safety architecture before enabling live automation.

Mission

Make recovery understandable and controlled.

The mission is to create a self-hosted reliability layer that can eventually detect failures, determine a policy-approved response, execute only authorized actions, verify recovery, roll back failed attempts, and preserve a clear incident record.

Today, that mission is represented by tested models, safety gates, and deterministic simulations—not a live production repair service.

Design principle

Safety before autonomy.

ShieldMendAi uses exact allowlists, approval policies, bounded attempts, cooldowns, circuit breakers, verification planning, rollback planning, redaction, and incident integrity checks.

There is no unrestricted “fix everything” mode. Production repair is unavailable.

Founder story

Built from the operational burden builders know.

Servers should not fail silently, and recovery should not depend on someone living in a terminal. ShieldMendAi is being built in public to turn that need into a reusable, inspectable product.
